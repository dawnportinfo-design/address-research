# Address Morphism Expansion Program

Address Morphism Theory is the core theory for mapping surface address expressions, evidence, country models, and identifiers to address referents. To make it a real research field rather than a single algorithm, it needs surrounding mathematical, AI, GIS, security, and distributed-systems theories.

## Priority Ten

1. Address Topology: neighborhoods, connectedness, boundaries, and continuity.
2. Address Semantics: meaning, equivalence, containment, and reference.
3. Address Algebra: composition, inverse, restriction, merge, and failure elements.
4. Address Information Theory: entropy, redundancy, ambiguity, and compression limits.
5. Address Metric Space: distance between expressions, referents, regions, and delivery capabilities.
6. Reversible Address Morphism: round-trip-safe transformations and loss certificates.
7. Probabilistic Address Morphism: candidate distributions, confidence, and manual-review thresholds.
8. Address Machine Translation: purpose-aware translation, transliteration, schema transfer, and verification.
9. Address Knowledge Graph: referents, names, evidence, routes, history, conflict, and reasoning.
10. Address Temporal Theory: valid time, transaction time, lineage, successor, and social continuity.

## Why These Matter

Without topology, AMT cannot discuss boundary stability or continuity.
Without semantics, it cannot distinguish same referent from similar string.
Without algebra, morphism chains cannot be checked compositionally.
Without information theory, identifier length and ambiguity reduction are guesswork.
Without metric spaces, candidate ranking and delivery feasibility remain ad hoc.
Without reversible and probabilistic morphisms, conversion and uncertainty are underspecified.
Without machine translation and knowledge graphs, multilingual and evidence-rich systems stay fragile.
Without temporal theory, administrative changes and address history cannot be reconstructed safely.

## Extended Research Fields

- Address category theory for commutative diagrams, schema evolution, and proof boundaries.
- Address cryptography for commitments, signatures, secret sharing, and authenticity.
- Address replication and synchronization theory for source updates and local-first consistency.
- Address spatial information theory for geohash, landforms, administrative polygons, and AGID cells.
- Address benchmark engineering for test vectors, corpora, quality metrics, and reproducibility.

## Research Rule

Every new field must answer four questions:

1. What AMT weakness does it solve?
2. What object, relation, or invariant does it introduce?
3. What can be tested without raw address data?
4. Which repository owns the implementation or proof artifact?

## Repository Boundary

This repository tracks the research map. Formal definitions and theorem statements can start here, but mature formal work should move into `address-morphism-theory`. ZK-specific proof work belongs in `zk-address-predicates`. Application and SDK demonstrations belong in `Address-Grid-ID`.
