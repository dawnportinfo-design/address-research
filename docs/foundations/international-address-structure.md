# International Address Structure Theory

International address systems differ by administrative hierarchy, postal-code maturity, language, script, delivery customs, and political boundaries.

## Core Question

Which abstractions survive across postal-rich, postal-weak, and no-postal-code countries?

## Research Tasks

1. Compare country models without forcing a single global form layout.
2. Separate administrative hierarchy from delivery capability.
3. Classify postal-rich, postal-weak, and no-postal-code systems.
4. Model contested or special regions as technical addressing scopes, not political claims.

## Safety Boundary

Use neutral technical identifiers. Do not claim sovereignty or political finality.
